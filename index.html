White Matter Tract Explorer

Interactive 3D Visualization of Human Brain Neuroanatomy

About This Project

Overview

The White Matter Tract Explorer is an interactive web-based tool for exploring and learning about white matter pathways in the human brain. This project visualizes 25+ major fiber tracts derived from diffusion MRI tractography data.

How to Use

  • Rotate: Click and drag to rotate the 3D model
  • Zoom: Scroll or pinch to zoom in/out
  • Pan: Right-click and drag (or two-finger drag on mobile)
  • Select Tract: Use the dropdown menu to view different white matter tracts
  • Show Brain: Toggle the transparent brain overlay for anatomical context
  • Dark Mode: Switch between light and dark themes for comfortable viewing
  • AR Mode: On compatible mobile devices, view models in augmented reality

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • i or ? - Open this info modal
  • Esc - Close modal
  • d - Toggle dark mode
  • b - Toggle brain overlay

White Matter Tracts

White matter consists of myelinated axon bundles that form communication pathways between different brain regions. Major tract categories include:

  • Association Tracts: Connect regions within the same hemisphere (e.g., Arcuate Fasciculus, Superior Longitudinal Fasciculus)
  • Commissural Tracts: Connect the two hemispheres (e.g., Corpus Callosum, Anterior Commissure)
  • Projection Tracts: Connect cortex with subcortical structures (e.g., Corticospinal Tract, Optic Radiation)

Data Source & Attribution

3D models are derived from high-resolution diffusion MRI tractography data from the Human Connectome Project. This visualization tool is intended for educational and research purposes.
